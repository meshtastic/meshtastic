## Verify Your Connection

This test confirms that your USB cable supports data transfer and that the correct serial drivers are installed.

**Before you start:** Connect your Meshtastic device to your computer with a USB cable.

<TabItem value="linux">

Open a terminal and run:

```shell
lsusb
```

Look for an entry like one of these:

```
ID xxxx:xxxx Silicon Labs CP210x UART Bridge
ID xxxx:xxxx QinHeng Electronics USB Single Serial
ID xxxx:xxxx Adafruit WisCore RAK4631 Board
```

If your device appears in the list, the driver is working correctly. ✅

</TabItem>
<TabItem value="macos">

Go to **Apple Menu  > About This Mac > More Info > System Report... > Hardware > USB**.

Look for an entry like one of these:

- `CP210X USB to UART Bridge Controller`
- `CH9102 USB to UART Bridge Controller`
- `WisCore RAK4631 Board`
- `USB Single Serial`

If your device appears, the driver is working correctly. ✅

</TabItem>
<TabItem value="windows">

Open **Device Manager** and expand **Ports (COM & LPT)**.

Look for an entry like one of these:

- `Silicon Labs CP210X USB to UART Bridge (COM5)`
- `Silicon Labs CH9102 USB to UART Bridge (COM5)`
- `USB-Enhanced-SERIAL CH9102 (COM5)`
- `USB Serial Device (COM5)`

If your device appears with a COM port number, the driver is working correctly. ✅

</TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Troubleshooting

If your device doesn't appear:

1. **Check your cable** — Many USB cables are charge-only and don't support data transfer. Try a different cable, or test yours by connecting it to a phone and copying a file.
2. **Try a different USB port** — Some ports may not provide enough power or have connection issues.
3. **Reinstall drivers** — Go back to the [serial driver installation page](/docs/getting-started/serial-drivers) and try installing a different driver.
4. **Reboot your computer** — Drivers may not take effect until after a restart.
