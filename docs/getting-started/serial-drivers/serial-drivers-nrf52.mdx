:::tip Most users don't need this page

nRF52, RP2040, and RP2350 devices use a UF2 bootloader that makes them appear as regular USB drives. **No drivers are needed** for flashing firmware — you can skip straight to [flashing](/docs/getting-started/flashing-firmware/nrf52/).

Only continue here if your device isn't being detected by your computer after connecting via USB.

:::

## Install CH34x Driver

Some nRF52-based devices (like the WisMesh Tag) use a CH34x USB-to-serial chip that may need a driver for serial communication (not for flashing).

:::info

Recent versions of macOS include a built-in USB serial driver. You likely don't need to install anything.

If you previously installed the CH34x driver (`CH341SER_MAC` / `CH34x_Install_V1.5.pkg`), **remove it** — it can cause conflicts:

1. Unplug your device.
2. Open Terminal and run:
   ```shell
   sudo rm -rf /Library/Extensions/usbserial.kext
   ```
3. Reboot your Mac.

:::

If you still need a driver: [CH34x Driver for macOS](https://github.com/WCHSoftGroup/ch34xser_macos)

</TabItem>

<TabItem value="windows">

- [CH34x Driver](http://www.wch-ic.com/downloads/CH341SER_EXE.html)

</TabItem>

:::important Reboot after installing

After installing any driver, **restart your computer** to complete the installation.

You can [test your serial driver installation](/docs/getting-started/serial-drivers/test-serial-driver-installation) to confirm everything is working.

:::
