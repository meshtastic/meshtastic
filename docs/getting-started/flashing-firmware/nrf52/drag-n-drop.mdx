Drag & drop is the simplest way to flash Meshtastic firmware on nRF52, RP2040, and RP2350 devices. The device appears as a USB drive and you copy a file to it — no special software needed.

:::tip Web Flasher Alternative

You can also use the [Meshtastic Web Flasher](https://flasher.meshtastic.org) to flash these devices directly from your browser.

:::

## Flash Firmware

### nRF52 Devices

1. Download and unzip the latest firmware from [Meshtastic Downloads](https://meshtastic.org/downloads).
2. Connect your device to your computer with a USB data cable.
3. **Double-click the reset button** on your device to enter bootloader mode.
4. A new USB drive will appear on your computer (Windows, Mac, Linux, or Android).
5. Open the drive — you should see files like `CURRENT.UF2`, `INDEX.HTM`, and `INFO_UF2.TXT`.
6. **Copy the correct `.uf2` firmware file** (e.g., `firmware-rak4631-X.X.X-xxxxxxx.uf2`) to this drive.
7. The device will automatically reboot and install the firmware once the file finishes copying.

### RP2040/RP2350 Devices

1. Download and unzip the latest firmware from [Meshtastic Downloads](https://meshtastic.org/downloads).
2. **Hold the BOOTSEL button** on your device, then connect it to your computer via USB while still holding the button.
3. A new USB drive will appear on your computer.
4. Open the drive — you should see `INDEX.HTM` and `INFO_UF2.TXT`.
5. **Copy the correct `.uf2` firmware file** to this drive.
6. The device will automatically reboot and install the firmware once the file finishes copying.

## Common Issues

:::info RAK4631 vs RAK4631-R

Before flashing, confirm whether you have a [RAK4631](https://docs.rakwireless.com/product-categories/wisblock/rak4631/overview) or a [RAK4631-R](https://docs.rakwireless.com/product-categories/wisblock/rak4631-r/overview). They look identical, but the RAK4631-R uses a different bootloader. If you have the -R variant, you'll need to [convert the bootloader](/docs/getting-started/flashing-firmware/nrf52/convert-rak4631r) first.

:::

:::info Device stuck in a crash loop?

Older versions of Meshtastic may leave stale data that causes problems after upgrading. If your device won't start after flashing, try a [factory erase](/docs/getting-started/flashing-firmware/nrf52/nrf52-erase) and then re-flash.

:::
