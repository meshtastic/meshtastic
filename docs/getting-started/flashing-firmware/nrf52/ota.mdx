Over-The-Air (OTA) updates let you flash firmware to nRF52 devices wirelessly via Bluetooth from a phone. This works for most RAK-based nRF52 devices. Older T-Echo bootloaders do not support OTA.

:::caution Higher risk than USB

OTA updates are more likely to fail than USB-based methods. If an OTA update fails, the device may become unresponsive and you'll need physical USB access to recover it. When possible, use [Drag & Drop](/docs/getting-started/flashing-firmware/nrf52/drag-n-drop) instead.

:::

<TabItem value="android">

:::caution Specific app version required

The current nRF DFU app (v2.3.0) is **not compatible** with Meshtastic. You must use the older **nRF Connect App version 4.24.3**, available from the [Nordic Semiconductor GitHub page](https://github.com/NordicSemiconductor/Android-nRF-Connect/releases/tag/v4.24.3). Newer versions of nRF Connect will not work.

:::

1. Download the firmware from the [Meshtastic Downloads](/downloads) page or [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/meshtastic/firmware/releases).
2. Unzip the firmware folder on your phone.
3. Install and open the **nRF Connect App v4.24.3**.
4. In the **Scanner** tab, find your device and tap **Connect**.
5. Tap the **DFU** icon in the top-right corner.
6. Confirm that **Distribution Packet (ZIP)** is selected and tap **OK**.
7. Select the correct firmware file (the one ending in `-ota.zip`).
8. The update will start automatically. **This will be slow** — be patient.
9. When complete, the device will reboot automatically.

</TabItem>

<TabItem value="apple">

OTA updates on iOS and iPadOS use the **nRF Device Firmware Update** app, available from the [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/nrf-device-firmware-update/id1624454660).

1. Download the firmware from the [Meshtastic Downloads](/downloads) page, [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/meshtastic/firmware/releases), or from within the Meshtastic iOS app.
2. Unzip the firmware folder.
3. Open the **nRF DFU** app and select the firmware file ending in `-ota.zip`.
4. Connect to your device.
5. Start the upload.

:::tip Prevent screen lock interruptions

Your iPhone's auto-lock can interrupt the Bluetooth upload. Either tap the screen periodically during the upload, or temporarily set **Settings > Display & Brightness > Auto-Lock** to **Never**.

:::

**If the upload fails**, try adjusting the packet settings in the nRF DFU app:
1. In the app settings, enable **Packets Receipt Notification**.
2. Change **Number of Packets** to a lower value (try **5**).

</TabItem>
