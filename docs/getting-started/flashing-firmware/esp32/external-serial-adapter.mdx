This is a last-resort method for when normal USB flashing doesn't work. Only use this if you've already tried the [Web Flasher](/docs/getting-started/flashing-firmware/esp32/web-flasher) and [CLI Script](/docs/getting-started/flashing-firmware/esp32/cli-script) without success.

:::danger Antenna Required!

**Never power on your radio without an antenna attached.** This can permanently damage the radio chip.

:::

## When You Might Need This

- Your device has a defective or non-standard USB-to-serial chip.
- Your device doesn't have a built-in USB-to-serial chip (e.g., some DIY builds like the [Hydra](https://github.com/Hydra-Designs/project-hydra-meshtastic-pcb)).
- The built-in USB connection isn't being recognized by your computer despite correct drivers.

## What You Need

A USB-to-serial adapter — we recommend one with a **Silicon Labs CP2102** chip, which is a reliable industry standard. These are widely available online.

## Setup Instructions

**Before connecting anything, unplug the adapter from your computer.**

1. Connect the **RX** pin of the adapter → **TX** pin of the device.
2. Connect the **TX** pin of the adapter → **RX** pin of the device.
3. Connect a **GND** pin of the adapter → **GND** pin of the device.
4. Connect **5V** (or 3.3V) of the adapter → matching voltage pin on the device.
5. **Bridge GPIO 0 to GND** on the device using a jumper wire. This puts the device into flash mode on the next power-up.

   Example wiring with a T-Beam:
   ![Wiring example](https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/9000580/168446438-d14f34a0-b92d-4716-87fd-e0c2927a35fd.png)

6. **Plug the adapter into your computer** via USB.
7. **Remove the jumper** from GPIO 0 to GND.

   ![Entering flash mode](https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/9000580/168446974-c31d3e9c-3397-4cb1-90a4-91ef2e4d5fac.gif)

## Flash the Firmware

Your device is now in flash mode. Follow the [CLI Script instructions](/docs/getting-started/flashing-firmware/esp32/cli-script) to flash the firmware using `esptool`.

After flashing is complete, press the **RST** (reset) button on the device if available.

To verify the flash was successful, you can connect using the Meshtastic Python CLI:

```shell
meshtastic --noproto
```

![Serial output confirmation](https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/9000580/168447159-71a6546a-f487-4bc2-86c1-4c489b2a8975.png)

## Troubleshooting

- **COM port permission errors**: Try running your terminal as administrator, or check that no other software (like Cura or Arduino IDE) is using the port.
- **Device not detected**: Verify your adapter's drivers are installed. On Windows, they usually install automatically — check Device Manager to confirm.
  ![Device Manager showing adapter](https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/9000580/168445869-f73667f5-0169-46fd-ae13-15c3abad31aa.png)
- **Still not working**: Try rebooting your computer, or use a different USB port.
